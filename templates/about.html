About Me

Who I Am

I'm Maxwell Burns, a junior at Baylor University studying Finance, Management Information Systems, and Business Analytics. I sit at the intersection of business and technology — comfortable in both a balance sheet and a code editor.

I'll be joining KPMG's Technology Assurance practice as an intern this summer, where I'll be supporting IT audit and risk advisory engagements. Outside of class and recruiting, I spend my time building real software: Django web applications, AI agents, and automation workflows that solve problems I actually care about.

Why I Build

My interest in technology started with a simple realization — most of the problems I saw in finance, leadership roles, and student organizations weren't strategy problems. They were workflow problems. Manual processes, scattered data, and tools that didn't talk to each other.

That's why I learned to code. As VP of Finance for Delta Sigma Pi, I rebuilt our entire budgeting process in Excel from scratch. Then I started asking bigger questions: What if I could build the tool myself? What would AI change about how this team operates? Those questions led me to Python, Django, and now to building AI-powered applications.

What I'm Looking For

Roles where I can bridge business strategy and technical execution — whether that's tech consulting, technology assurance, product, or any seat where understanding both sides is an advantage. I work best with people who value clear communication, curiosity over credentials, and shipping real things over talking about them.

Outside of Work

When I'm not in the library or behind a screen, I'm usually in the gym, on a pickleball court, or editing video.
